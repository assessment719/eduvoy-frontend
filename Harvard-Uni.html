Harvard University

Cambridge, MA

Study in the Harvard University


Harvard University is located in Cambridge, Massachusetts, just across the Charles River from Boston. It stands as one of the most prestigious and well-known institutions of higher learning worldwide. Founded in 1636, it is the oldest university in the United States, renowned for academic excellence and groundbreaking research. The campus, situated in the heart of Greater Boston, offers a blend of historic buildings and modern facilities. Harvard’s community of over 20,000 students hails from over 150 countries, ensuring a truly global learning environment that is both enriching and stimulating.

A Brief History:


Harvard University was established in 1636, making it the oldest institution of higher education in the United States. Originally founded to train clergy, the university rapidly expanded its academic offerings to encompass a wide array of disciplines. Named after its first benefactor, John Harvard, the institution has played a pivotal role in shaping higher education in the U.S. and globally. Over the years, it has evolved into a world-leading research university, producing leaders and thinkers who have significantly impacted politics, business, science, and the arts.

Culture and Traditions:


Harvard is deeply rooted in history and tradition while embracing a progressive outlook. It prides itself on fostering a diverse and inclusive environment where students from all backgrounds collaborate and share ideas. Key traditions include the annual Harvard-Yale football game, which has been held since 1875, and the iconic Harvard Commencement held each May in Harvard Yard. The university’s culture encourages intellectual curiosity, debate, and innovation, making it a hub for creative and scholarly pursuits.

Famous Places to Visit on Campus:


Harvard’s campus is rich with landmarks that reflect its storied legacy. Harvard Yard, the oldest part of the university, serves as a central gathering spot for students and faculty. Widener Library houses millions of books and is a centerpiece for academic research. The Harvard Art Museums showcase a diverse collection of artworks from different eras and cultures. Memorial Hall honors Harvard alumni who fought in the Civil War and features striking Gothic architecture. Harvard Square, located near the campus, is a bustling area filled with cafes, bookstores, and venues that reflect the vibrant student life.


The Education System:


Harvard offers a robust and flexible education system designed to encourage intellectual exploration and academic rigor. Harvard provides over 3,700

courses in more than 50 undergraduate concentrations. Its graduate and professional schools, including law, medicine, and business, offer world-class education aimed at producing leaders in various fields. Personalized academic advising and tutoring are integral parts of the Harvard educational experience, ensuring that students have the support they need.

Top-Ranked Schools and Colleges:


Harvard is home to several world-renowned schools and colleges. Harvard College provides undergraduate education that emphasizes critical thinking and intellectual exploration. Harvard Business School (HBS) is a leader in business education, offering an MBA program known for shaping the world’s top business minds. Harvard Law School (HLS) produces some of the most influential legal scholars and practitioners. Harvard Medical School (HMS) leads in medical education and research, while the Harvard Kennedy School is recognized for its contributions to public policy and governance.

Notable Alumni:


Harvard boasts an impressive alumni network that includes world leaders, innovators, and changemakers. Former U.S. Presidents Barack Obama and John F. Kennedy both studied at Harvard. Technology magnates like Bill Gates (co-founder of Microsoft) and Mark Zuckerberg (founder of Facebook) also have ties to the university. Ruth Bader Ginsburg, a former U.S. Supreme Court Justice, is another notable Harvard alum. These individuals exemplify the university’s legacy of producing graduates who shape the future in various fields.

Growth of International Students:


Harvard is a magnet for international students, with approximately 23% of its student body coming from outside the United States. Students from over 150 countries choose Harvard for its academic excellence, global
reputation, and commitment to fostering a multicultural community. The university’s extensive financial aid program ensuresthat talented students from all economic backgrounds have access to its world-class education. Harvard’s resources for international students, including language support and career advising, make it a welcoming destination for students from across the globe.

Future Job Markets:


Harvard continues to prepare its students for the future by emphasizing emerging fields such as artificial intelligence, biotechnology, and sustainability. As the job market evolves, Harvard’s focus on innovation and interdisciplinary education ensures that its graduates remain competitive in dynamic industries. The university’s entrepreneurial ecosystem, supported by initiatives like the Harvard Innovation Labs, encourages students to pursue cutting-edge research and start new ventures, positioning them to lead in tomorrow’s job markets.

Harvard University: Excellence in Rankings and Accolades


1. Global Ranking Powerhouse: Consistently ranked in the top 2 globally by QS and Times Higher Education, Harvard sets the benchmark for academic excellence worldwide.

2. Home to Nobel Laureates: With over 160 Nobel Prize winners, Harvard’s groundbreaking contributions across various fields solidify its position as a global research leader.

3. Rhodes Scholar Legacy: Harvard has nurtured over 370 Rhodes Scholars, making it a top institution for producing global leaders and Oxford-bound scholars.

4. Leading in Graduate Employability: Ranked high in QS Graduate Employability, Harvard’s prestigious reputation and robust alumni network ensure top placements for its graduates worldwide.

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements:


• Undergraduate Degree: Applicants must hold a bachelor’s degree or its equivalent from an accredited institution.

• GPA: Although no specific GPA is required, successful applicants generally have a GPA of 3.5 or higher on a 4.0 scale.

• Prerequisite Courses: Some programs may require prior coursework or relevant academic background in the chosen field of study.

• Standardized Tests:

        •	       - Graduate Programs: GRE/GMAT may be required depending on the specific graduate program.

        •	       - GProfessional Schools: LSAT for Law, MCAT for Medicine, etc.
• Work Experience: Some professional programs, especially MBA and public policy courses, require applicants to have relevant work experience (typically 2-5 years).

• Letters of Recommendation: Usually, two to three letters from academic or professional references, depending on the program.

English Language Requirements:


• TOEFL: Minimum score of 100 on the Internet-based test (iBT).

• IELTS: Minimum overall band score of 7.5.

• PTE Academic: Accepted by some departments, with a minimum score of 70.

• Waiver: Applicants may be eligible for a waiver if they have completed their degree in an institution where English is the primary language of instruction.

Note: English proficiency test requirements vary slightly based on the department, so it’s recommended to check specific program details.

Mandatory Documents:


• Academic Transcripts: Official transcripts from all previously attended institutions.

• Statement of Purpose (SOP): A detailed essay outlining academic goals, research interests, and career objectives.

• Resume/CV: A current resume that highlights academic achievements, professional experience, and relevant skills.

• Letters of Recommendation: Generally two to three letters from previous professors, employers, or other professional contacts.

• Standardized Test Scores: GRE, GMAT, LSAT, MCAT, or others (as per the program).

• English Language Test Scores: TOEFL/IELTS/PTE (for non-native English speakers).

• Application Fee: A non-refundable fee is usually required; this can vary depending on the program.
